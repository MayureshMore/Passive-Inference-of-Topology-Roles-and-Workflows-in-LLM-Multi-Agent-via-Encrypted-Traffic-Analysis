# Data archive — frozen features & raw captures

The `data/` directory is **gitignored** (raw pcaps, processed feature matrices,
and model checkpoints are not in the repo). To reproduce the paper's tables and
figures you download a published data archive and unpack it into `data/`.

> **The committed `data/results/` is the canonical, frozen paper output.** It is
> the one thing that is *not* regenerated by reproduction: `scripts/reproduce.sh`
> writes to a sandbox (`data/results_demo/`) and compares against it, and a full
> re-run writes to a fresh copy (`A2A_RESULTS_DIR=...`). Nothing in the normal
> workflow overwrites `data/results/`.

## Published archives

| Archive | Contents | Size (approx) | Needed for |
|---|---|---|---|
| `a2a-features.zip` | `data/processed*/` — the `.npz` feature matrices + `labels.json` for every deployment (A, B, amodel, blogic), background, defended sets, and the C5 WAN capture | small (tens of MB) | **all of `reproduce.sh`** except live-defense overhead |
| `a2a-raw-pcaps.zip` | `data/raw*/` — header-only `.pcap` captures (96-byte snaplen, metadata only, no payload) + `.json` label sidecars | large (GBs) | live-defense **byte/latency overhead** only, and re-extracting features from scratch |

**Archive location:** `<ARCHIVE_URL — to be filled in after the Zenodo/figshare/OSF upload>`

Most reproduction needs only `a2a-features.zip`. Download the raw-pcap archive
only if you want the C4 defense *overhead* numbers (which are measured from the
wire) or intend to re-run feature extraction.

## Unpack

From the repository root:

```bash
# features only (enough for the deterministic table/figure reproduction)
unzip a2a-features.zip -d .        # creates data/processed*/...

# optional: raw captures (only for defense-overhead numbers / re-extraction)
unzip a2a-raw-pcaps.zip -d .       # creates data/raw*/...
```

After unpacking, the layout matches what the scripts expect:

```
data/
  processed/                 deployment A (llama3.2:3b) — the headline
  processed_b_sdk/           deployment B (qwen2.5:7b)  — cross-deployment
  processed_amodel_sdk/      A-logic + qwen2.5:7b       — model-vs-logic
  processed_blogic_sdk/      B-logic + llama3.2:3b      — model-vs-logic
  processed_background_sdk/  real background traffic    — open-world
  processed_wan/             US⇄India WAN capture       — C5
  processed_defense_rate/    live "rate" defense
  processed_defense_pad/     live "pad" defense
  raw*/                      (only if you unpacked a2a-raw-pcaps.zip)
  results/                   committed in the repo — the frozen canonical results
```

Then follow **Reproducing the paper** in [README.md](README.md).

## Re-packaging (maintainer)

To rebuild the archives from a populated `data/` directory:

```bash
bash scripts/package_data.sh            # writes dist/a2a-features.zip + dist/a2a-raw-pcaps.zip
```

Upload the two zips to the data host, then paste the DOI/URL into the
**Archive location** line above and into the header of `scripts/reproduce.sh`.

## Provenance & ethics

- Captures are **metadata only**: tcpdump ran with a 96-byte snaplen and a BPF
  filter scoped to the agent host:port flows. No payload bytes were ever written.
- All traffic was generated on infrastructure owned by the researchers; no
  external systems were targeted.
- Raw collection is stochastic (LLM generation over a live network), so a fresh
  collection produces a *different* dataset with point estimates inside the
  published CIs — it does not reproduce the exact numbers. Deterministic
  reproduction is of the **analysis** from the frozen features, via
  `scripts/reproduce.sh`.
